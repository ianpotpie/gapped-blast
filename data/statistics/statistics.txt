Question 4: How might these statistics be relevant to the BLAST algorithm?

The fact that random alignment scores have a tight cluster and are low-scoring is good for our BLAST implementation,
because it is unlikely that a high-scoring alignment would result from random alignments, and thus a high-scoring
alignment is indicative of a true alignment match, rather than being coincidental. We can also use the statistics from
these random alignment scores in order to pick our falloff and cutoff values in order to help guarantee true matches
in our BLAST algorithm.
